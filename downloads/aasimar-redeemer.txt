 <p><b>AC</b> 23 (25 with shield raised); <b>Fort</b> +12, <b>Ref</b> +8, <b>Will</b> +11: +1 status to all saves vs. disease (against diseases, critical failures become failures)</p> 
 <p><b>HP</b> 73</p> 

<div class="special-abilities">
<p><b>Divine Grace</b> [reaction] <b>Trigger</b> The angelkin is targeted by a spell that allows a saving throw.; <b>Effect</b> The scion gains a +2 circumstance bonus to the saving throw.</p> 
<p><b>Glimpse of Redemption</b> [reaction] <b>Trigger</b> An enemy damages one of the angelkin&#8217;s allies. Both the enemy and ally must be within 15 feet of the angelkin. <b>Effect</b> The angelkin causes its foe to hesitate under the weight of its sins as visions of possible redemption play out in its mind&#8217;s eye. The foe chooses one of two options:</p> 
<ul>
  <li>The ally is completely unharmed by the triggering damage.</li> 
  <li>The ally gains resistance 7 to all damage against the triggering damage. After the damaging effect resolves, the enemy becomes enfeebled 2 until the end of its next turn.</li> 
 </ul> 
 <p><b>Shield Block</b> [reaction] <b>Trigger</b> The angelkin has its shield raised and takes damage from a physical attack. <b>Effect</b> The angelkin snaps its shield into place to deflect a blow. The shield prevents the angelkin from taking an amount of damage up to the shield’s Hardness. The angelkin and the shield each take any remaining damage, possibly breaking or destroying the shield.</p>
</div>

<p class="header">About</p>
<p>Mortals whose <a href="https://pf2.d20pfsrd.com/rules/ancestries/">ancestry</a> has been influenced by celestials are known as aasimars, and angelkin, who have blood of angels coursing through their veins, are among the most common type of them. Many angelkin seek adventure as a means of doing good in the world.</p>
